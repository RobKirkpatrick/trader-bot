# Brief Description

Public Sentiment Trader runs on Public.com's API and was built for the investor who has a job and a life — but still wants to act on the market intelligently, not reactively.

Markets generate more signal than any part-time investor can process. News is dramatic by design. r/WallStreetBets is sarcastic and gambling-inclined. r/stocks skews slow. SEC 8-K filings require legal interpretation. Combine them and you have a firehose of conflicting, biased data arriving faster than any human can synthesize.

Claude reads six signal sources simultaneously — price action, news sentiment, macro headlines, entity-level analysis, keyword scoring, and social pulse — weights them dynamically, and produces a single score per ticker with a plain-English rationale and a recommended action.

The throttle is yours. At the conservative end: a nightly digest email with three annotated picks, nothing executing without your click. At the aggressive end: a fully autonomous bot scanning four times daily, executing at market open, and flagging options plays for one-click approval.

Public.com's commission-free order execution, real-time quotes API, and options chain data with Greeks make professional-grade market access available without a Bloomberg terminal. A Kalshi side strategy covers compute costs — keeping the Sentiment Trader effectively free to run.
